# The History of the Protestant Reformation

The Protestant Reformation was a significant movement in the 16th century that led to the establishment of Protestantism as a separate branch of Christianity. It was initiated by Martin Luther, a German theologian and monk.

{/* truncate */}

## Martin Luther

Martin Luther was a key figure in the Protestant Reformation. He was born in 1483 in Germany and became a Catholic monk. However, Luther became disillusioned with the corruption and abuses he witnessed within the Catholic Church, particularly the sale of indulgences.

In 1517, Luther famously nailed his Ninety-Five Theses to the door of the Castle Church in Wittenberg, Germany. This act of protest against the Catholic Church's practices sparked a widespread movement for reform.
